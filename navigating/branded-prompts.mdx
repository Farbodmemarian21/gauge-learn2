## Summary

<Note>
  This lesson covers Branded Prompts — the queries that directly name your brand — and how to use Gauge to monitor and improve what AI engines say when someone asks about you specifically.
</Note>

**Key Concepts:**
- What branded prompts are and why they matter
- How to add and manage branded prompts in Gauge
- Reading AI-generated answers to branded queries
- Identifying and correcting inaccurate or unfavorable brand narratives

## Overview

### What Are Branded Prompts?

[Explain branded prompts as queries that include your brand name directly — for example, "What is [Brand]?", "Is [Brand] good for X?", "How does [Brand] compare to [Competitor]?". These queries represent what potential customers are asking AI tools about you specifically.]

### Why Branded Prompts Matter

[Explain that when someone asks an AI tool directly about your brand, the response they get shapes their perception before they ever visit your website. A poor, incomplete, or inaccurate AI response to a branded query is a direct threat to conversion.]

### Adding Branded Prompts

[Walk through how to add branded prompts to your Gauge tracking. Recommend a core set to start with: brand definition queries, comparison queries, and use-case specific queries.]

### Reading and Auditing AI Responses

[Explain how to view the actual AI-generated answers to your branded prompts across different AI engines. Walk through what to look for — accuracy, completeness, tone, whether competitors are being recommended alongside or instead of your brand.]

### Addressing Inaccurate or Unfavorable Responses

[Explain the strategies available when AI responses about your brand are inaccurate or unfavorable — publishing clear, authoritative content that directly addresses the gaps, earning press coverage, and building more citation-worthy resources.]

<Tip>
  Set up alerts for your most important branded prompts so you're notified immediately if the AI response changes significantly — for example, if a competitor starts appearing in the answer to "What is [Brand]?".
</Tip>
