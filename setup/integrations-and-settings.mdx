## Summary

<Note>
  This lesson walks you through setting up your Gauge workspace — connecting integrations, configuring your brand profile, and adding the prompts you want to track.
</Note>

**Key Concepts:**
- Connecting your brand profile and competitors
- Adding the prompts Gauge will monitor
- Configuring notification and reporting settings
- Available integrations (Slack, etc.)

## Overview

### Setting Up Your Brand Profile

[Explain how to set up your brand profile in Gauge — entering your brand name, website, and key competitors you want to track against. This is the foundation of all your AEO monitoring.]

### Adding Prompts to Track

[Explain how to add the prompts you want Gauge to monitor. Walk through best practices for choosing prompts — mix of industry queries, comparison queries, and use-case specific searches that your target customers are likely asking in AI tools.]

### Adding Competitors

[Explain how to add competitor brands to your Gauge workspace so you can benchmark your AEO performance against them in rankings and visibility.]

### Configuring Notifications

[Describe the notification options available — email digests, Slack alerts for significant changes in visibility or rankings, and report frequency settings.]

### Slack Integration

[Walk through how to connect Gauge to your Slack workspace so your team receives AEO updates directly in a channel of your choice.]

<Warning>
  Make sure to add at least 5–10 prompts before your first data pull — the more prompts you track, the more comprehensive your visibility picture will be.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Not sure which prompts to add? Think about how your customers would ask an AI tool about the problem your product solves — not your brand name, but the problem itself.
</Tip>
