## Summary

<Note>
  This lesson introduces the core metrics you'll see throughout Gauge — what they mean and how to use them to evaluate your AEO performance.
</Note>

**Key Concepts:**
- Visibility: how often your brand appears in AI-generated answers
- Rankings: where your brand ranks relative to competitors in AI responses
- Citations: when an AI engine links or refers to your content as a source
- Prompts: the specific queries being tracked to monitor your presence
- Branded Prompts: prompts that directly mention your brand name

## Overview

### Visibility

[Explain visibility as the core metric — the percentage of relevant AI-generated answers in which your brand appears in some form. Higher visibility means your brand is more present in the answers your target audience is seeing.]

### Rankings

[Explain how Gauge ranks brands within AI responses. When an AI engine mentions multiple brands in response to a query, Gauge tracks the order and frequency to give you a relative ranking against competitors.]

### Citations

[Explain citations — when an AI engine directly links to or credits a specific URL or source from your brand. Citations are a strong signal of authority and are tracked separately from general mentions.]

### Prompts

[Explain that Gauge monitors a defined set of prompts — queries that represent how your target customers are searching in AI tools. These can be industry questions, comparison queries, or use-case specific searches.]

### Branded Prompts

[Explain branded prompts — prompts that include your brand name directly, such as "What does [Brand] do?" or "Is [Brand] good for X?" These are tracked to monitor brand perception and direct brand queries in AI tools.]

<Tip>
  Start by reviewing your Visibility and Rankings scores together — a high visibility score with a low ranking means you're being mentioned but not recommended first.
</Tip>
