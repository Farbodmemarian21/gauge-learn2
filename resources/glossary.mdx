## AEO Glossary

A reference for the core terms used in Answer Engine Optimization and throughout the Gauge platform.

---

### AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

The practice of optimizing a brand's presence in AI-generated answers produced by answer engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google Gemini. AEO is to AI search what SEO is to traditional search — a set of strategies to improve how and how often your brand appears in relevant responses.

---

### Answer Engine

An AI-powered tool that responds to user queries with synthesized, conversational answers rather than a list of links. Examples include ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google Gemini, Claude, and Microsoft Copilot. Answer engines are increasingly how people research brands, products, and services.

---

### Citation

When an AI engine directly references or links to a specific URL or source as the basis for part of its response. Citations are a strong signal that the AI engine treats that content as authoritative. Earning citations is one of the primary goals of an AEO content strategy.

---

### Prompt

A query or question submitted to an AI answer engine. In the context of Gauge, "prompts" refers to the specific queries your team has chosen to monitor — the questions your target customers are likely asking AI tools about your industry, use cases, or brand.

---

### Visibility

A Gauge metric that measures the percentage of your tracked prompts for which your brand appears in the AI-generated answer. A visibility score of 70% means your brand appears in 70 out of every 100 responses to your tracked prompts.

---

### Rankings

A Gauge metric that tracks where your brand appears relative to competitors when multiple brands are mentioned in an AI-generated answer. A ranking of 1 means your brand is mentioned first; higher numbers indicate your brand appears later in the response or less prominently.

---

### Branded Prompt

A prompt that directly names your brand — for example, "What does [Brand] do?" or "Is [Brand] good for [use case]?". Branded prompts are tracked separately because they represent direct brand perception queries, where the AI's response has an outsized influence on potential customers.

### Branded Prompt Monitoring

The practice of tracking AI-generated responses to queries that include your brand name, to ensure accuracy, completeness, and a favorable narrative.

---

### Citation Gap

A prompt where a competitor is being cited by AI engines but your brand is not. Citation gaps represent high-priority content opportunities — creating or improving content that could earn a citation for that prompt.

---

### LLM (Large Language Model)

The underlying AI technology that powers answer engines. LLMs like GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini are trained on large datasets and generate responses by predicting the most relevant and coherent answer to a query. The content and sources they were trained on, plus real-time retrieval, influence what they say about any given brand.

---

### Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

A technique used by many answer engines where the AI retrieves real-time web content to supplement its training data before generating a response. RAG means that current, well-structured web content can influence AI responses even after the model's training cutoff date.
